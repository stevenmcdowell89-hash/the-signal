# The Signal — Compliance Checklist

Run through every item before delivering. Fix any failures before sharing.

---

## Volume & Richness
- [ ] At least 5,000 words (target 6,000–8,000+)
- [ ] 20–30 rendered pages
- [ ] Every major section has at least one relevant image
- [ ] Sections are visually distinct at a glance — clear backgrounds, dividers, spacing

## Coverage (most common failure mode)
- [ ] **Tournament priority check:** if WC qualifiers, Euros, WC finals, or CL/EL knockouts are active this week, they lead The Touchline — domestic leagues are secondary or omitted
- [ ] When no major tournament: Serie A top 6 AND PL top 6 league tables present, Serie A ≥ PL in depth, coverage beyond Juve
- [ ] CL/EL results included when played (knockouts lead over domestic)
- [ ] Golf/major sport events get proper coverage when in season
- [ ] Release Radar has 15–20+ items across ALL categories (film, TV, games, LEGO, tech, books, music)
- [ ] Star Wars is mentioned
- [ ] Books content present
- [ ] This Week in History present with pre-WW2 preference
- [ ] No coverage gaps: Android/Pixel/Xiaomi, Switch 2/Steam Deck, LEGO, synthwave, fitness
- [ ] Issue has news AND features/evergreen — not purely news

## Structure
- [ ] Touchline leads with data (tables, scores) BEFORE narrative
- [ ] Foreword is 50–80 words, one thread only
- [ ] No "Other Signals" section — items distributed to relevant "Also" lists
- [ ] On the Radar has NO overlap with Release Radar
- [ ] LEGO in Pixel & Byte, not Screen & Sound
- [ ] History prefers ancient/medieval/classical, not modern/WW2
- [ ] Music covered only when relevant — not a fixed section
- [ ] Session has only sourced content or is omitted entirely

## Voice & Tone
- [ ] No "what this means for you" framing
- [ ] No "your son will love this" in prose — family recs in dedicated sidebars only
- [ ] Opinions present throughout — not neutral press releases
- [ ] Football reads as editorial, not match reports
- [ ] No spoilers for any book or show — ever
- [ ] Writes like a magazine journalist, not a personal assistant

## Visual Variety
- [ ] 8+ different component types used
- [ ] No two consecutive sections use the same layout pattern
- [ ] No 3+ screen-heights of unbroken prose
- [ ] At least 3 pull quotes across the issue
- [ ] At least 2 big-number callouts or display stats
- [ ] 3–5 Did You Know boxes, section-aware colours
- [ ] `.reveal` class on key elements (headers, stats, images, angles)
- [ ] Read-next connectors between at least 3 major sections
- [ ] At least one split layout, one card stack or also-cards, one image montage or offset

## Wildcards & Synthesis
- [ ] 2–3 items the reader didn't ask for
- [ ] 2 of 8 Long Shelf items are wildcards
- [ ] Cross-cluster connections present where natural

## Technical
- [ ] Navigator cards anchor-link to sections with matching `id` attributes
- [ ] CSS from styles.css is in a `<style>` tag
- [ ] JS from script.js is in a `<script>` tag before `</body>`
- [ ] Progress bar and back-to-top button functional
- [ ] All sources linked
- [ ] No AI-generated images, no fabricated URLs
