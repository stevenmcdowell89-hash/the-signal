The Strait That Blinked — Then Closed Again

Oil tanker near Basra, Iraq, as the Strait of Hormuz standoff enters its latest phase. Credit: Reuters / Al Jazeera
  • Iran reopened the Strait of Hormuz to commercial traffic on Friday — and closed it again on Saturday
  • IRGC gunboats fired on two vessels attempting to cross; India summoned Iran's ambassador over attacks on its crude carriers
  • The two-week ceasefire — brokered by Pakistan on April 8 — expires Wednesday. No date set for a second round of talks
Key Numbers
  • 20% — share of world oil through the Strait
  • ~3,000 — Iranian deaths in the conflict
  • 13 — US service members killed
  • ~80 — energy facilities damaged in the region
  • 3 — US aircraft carriers currently in the Gulf
  • 4 days — until ceasefire expiry as of Saturday

On Friday, there was a moment of genuine optimism. Iran's foreign minister declared the Strait of Hormuz "completely open." Oil prices fell nearly 9%, the biggest single-day drop since the war began in late February. US President Donald Trump said things were going "along very well." A second round of Islamabad talks seemed hours away.

Then Trump reaffirmed that the US naval blockade — which prevents ships from entering or departing Iranian ports — would "remain in full force" until a deal was done. Iran's Supreme Leader Khamenei responded that the country's navy would "make their enemies taste the bitterness of new defeats." By Saturday morning, the IRGC's joint military command declared that "control of the Strait of Hormuz has reverted to its prior condition under stringent management and oversight." IRGC gunboats then fired on an Indian crude carrier and a container vessel attempting to transit.

The economic damage is already structural. The International Energy Agency estimates approximately 10% of global oil supply has been shut down. Over 80 energy facilities in the region have sustained damage. Analysts say restoring production to pre-war levels could take up to two years even with an immediate agreement. Oil prices, which briefly touched $90 per barrel on Friday, climbed back toward $97 by Saturday evening. Jet fuel and natural gas shortages are expected to persist in some markets regardless of outcome.

The Angle

The duelling blockades are the story here. The US demands the Strait reopen before negotiations can progress; Iran demands the naval blockade be lifted before the Strait reopens. Neither side can afford to go first. That deadlock makes a clean diplomatic resolution before Wednesday's ceasefire expiry look increasingly unlikely. A third outcome — a rolling series of short extensions with incremental tension releases — now seems more probable than either a deal or a resumption of full hostilities. But the gap between those outcomes is enormous, and the Strait of Hormuz is the fulcrum either way.

Capcom on Switch 2: Pragmata Lands, Imperfect but Worth It

9/10
Nintendo Life's score for Pragmata on Switch 2 — "another excellent Capcom port"

Capcom has made an art of treating Nintendo's hybrid as a first-class platform. After the RE Engine's successful run with the Resident Evil trilogy, expectations were high for Pragmata, the studio's ambitious new sci-fi IP arriving day-and-date on Switch 2 on April 17. The verdict: brilliant game, honest performance trade-offs.

Switch 2 Version

  • 540p internal (handheld), DLSS upscaled to 1080p docked
  • Unlocked framerate — 30–40fps in busy scenes, ~50fps indoors
  • Simplified shadows and textures vs. PS5
  • Strand hair replaced with rigid card rendering
  • VRR in handheld helps smooth the choppier moments
  • Verdict: Fully playable, worth it if Switch 2 is your platform

PS5 Version

  • Full 4K/60fps with high-fidelity lighting and effects
  • Strand-based hair physics
  • Full shadow maps and texture detail
  • The definitive visual experience
  • Not portable
  • Verdict: Best-looking; Switch 2 owners won't feel hard done by

The game itself is one of the surprises of 2026. Pragmata centres on Hugh, a space-suit-wearing mercenary trapped on a disintegrating moon base, and Diana, a small android who rides on his back and provides hacking abilities. The combat system — simultaneously shooting with Hugh while guiding Diana's hacking grid via face buttons — creates a genuinely fresh tension that, reportedly, never gets boring even as enemy complexity ramps up. TheSixthAxis calls it "one of the highlights of 2026."

'Awful April' and the New Tax Year

The tax year resets every April 6. This one brought more changes than most — and not all of them in your favour.

The 2026/27 tax year opened on April 6 with a raft of changes that AJ Bell has collectively labelled "Awful April." The state pension rose 4.8% under the triple lock — the new full pension is £241.30/week, up from £230.25. That sounds positive until you notice the personal allowance remains frozen at £12,570 until 2031, meaning the full state pension now sits within touching distance of the tax-free threshold. The government has promised that from April 2027, no one with only state pension income will pay tax — but in the meantime, more pensioners will be pulled into paying income tax than at any point in recent history.

Key Changes — April 2026
  • Dividend tax up 2% — now 10.75% (basic rate), 35.75% (higher/additional)
  • IHT on farm/business assets — full relief up to £2.5m; 50% above that (was 100%)
  • AIM shares IHT relief halved — from 100% to 50% business property relief
  • Making Tax Digital — sole traders/landlords over £50k income now file quarterly
  • State pension age rise begins — phased increase to 67 by April 2028
  • Energy bills up — April's bills increase lands alongside frozen wages for many

The ISA allowance stays at £20,000 for 2026/27 — but this is effectively the last full year under-65s can use the complete cash ISA allowance. From April 2027, that drops to £12,000 for cash ISAs (the full £20,000 can still go into stocks and shares). The stated rationale is pushing savers toward UK equities investment, though critics note it simply removes flexibility. If you have unused cash you want to shelter from tax, the window to put the full £20k into a cash ISA closes April 5, 2027.

"Every pound placed in an ISA grows without being taxed, which can significantly impact long-term returns. For those with excess savings outside tax-protected accounts, increasing an ISA balance before the tax year concludes can be one of the simplest methods to ensure their funds generate interest."

— Andrew Prosser, Head of Investments, InvestEngine

The pension picture is complex but important: the annual allowance remains £60,000 (or your earnings, whichever is lower), and carry-forward lets you contribute from up to three previous tax years — potentially £220,000 if you've left all three years unused. From April 2027, unspent pensions will lose IHT exemption, so anyone using a SIPP as an inheritance vehicle has roughly twelve months to review their estate planning. The interactive investor/AJ Bell consensus: the change most likely to shift behaviour is the IHT on pensions, not the ISA cut.

One Thing to Do This Month

If you haven't contributed to your 2026/27 ISA yet, the new tax year's allowance is now live. Consider splitting between cash and stocks & shares before next April's cash ISA cap kicks in. This is also a good moment to review any old ISAs paying below current rates — you can transfer without losing tax-free status. YBS guide →

The Night Madrid's Resistance Broke

Real Madrid arrived in Munich having won the first leg 2–1, and for 90 minutes they nearly pulled off a remontada worthy of their greatest European nights. Arda Güler — in just 35 seconds — lobbed Manuel Neuer from distance with the fastest Champions League goal of the season. Güler then made it 2–0 Real on the night with a stunning free kick. Harry Kane equalised from inside the box. Kylian Mbappé restored the lead in first-half stoppage time. At 3–2 on the night (4–4 aggregate), Real were heading through.

Then Eduardo Camavinga received a second yellow card in the 86th minute for two needless challenges. Ten men. Three minutes later, Luis Díaz thundered one in from range to make it 3–3 — 5–4 on aggregate, Bayern through. In the 90th minute, Michael Olise added the cherry. The final: 4–3. Aggregate: 6–4. Real Madrid are out.

The Take

Camavinga's red card may be the most costly disciplinary moment in Real Madrid's European season in years. The result was mathematically possible without it — but the ten-man final minutes turned an extraordinary game into a historic upset. Vincent Kompany has now eliminated the reigning European champions. Arsenal, meanwhile, qualified with a 0–0 — efficient, unspectacular, and entirely intentional from Arteta. The semi-final draw couldn't be more evenly matched.

What Actually Sells on Etsy in 2026

  • PNG/SVG design files dominate — high volume, repeat buyers, scales with listings
  • Bundles consistently outsell single products at every price point
  • Notion templates: growing but niche-dependent — the generic ones aren't moving

There is a version of the Etsy digital products story that goes: "create once, earn forever." That version is true but incomplete. The sellers generating consistent monthly income aren't running 10 perfect listings — they're running 300+ listings across tightly niched product sets, and they reached that number by understanding what the platform's algorithm actually rewards: transaction velocity and listing count.

The categories with the strongest 2026 data are PNG clipart sets and SVG cut files — primarily for Cricut and Silhouette users who buy in volume and return repeatedly. These are not glamorous products, but they're durable. Tumbler wrap designs and mug wrap files are running at similar velocity. The economics: a 50-file SVG bundle at £8 outperforms ten individual files at £1 in both revenue and algorithmic weight per transaction.

"Volume compounds passive income — one seller in our community went from 12 listings to 400+ in a month by batch-creating design sets. The algorithm rewards active shops."

— MyDesigns research roundup, 2026

Notion Templates — The Honest Picture

The top Notion template creator (Easlo) has earned over $500,000. A handful of sellers report $1,500+/month from a single template on the Notion marketplace. On Etsy specifically, the picture is more modest — one seller on Reddit reported £640 profit in their most active year, noting that without active Pinterest promotion, sales become sporadic. The consensus from practitioners: Notion templates work on Etsy if you're in a specific niche (fantasy writers, ADHD planning, wedding planning, freelance business systems) rather than generic productivity. "Life planner" and "second brain" templates are now crowded; the gap is in verticals most creators haven't built for yet.

Pricing Insight

Notion templates on Etsy: $8–25 for individual templates, more for bundles. Resume templates (Canva): $5–15. Wedding invitation suites: $15–60. The productivity/template niche punches above its weight on AOV.

The SEO Reality

Etsy's algorithm favours shops with high listing counts and steady transaction velocity. Digital products are the fastest way to build volume because fulfilment is automated — no inventory, no shipping, no production delays.

The sellers who treat digital products as a catalogue business — rather than a one-hit-product business — are the ones building durable income. Two Weeks Out: Why the Taper Works (And Why You Have to Trust It)

Taper structure example — volume reduces while intensity is maintained. Credit: WHOOP
41–60%
Volume reduction shown to maximise race performance in Bosquet et al. — across five endurance sports, this range outperforms both larger and smaller cuts

Two weeks before a 10k race is precisely the window where the taper begins in earnest — and where the most common mistake is made. That mistake is not trusting it. The body takes 7 to 10 days to adapt to any given training stimulus. Once you're within that window, you cannot add to your fitness. What you can do is show up to the start line with all your previous adaptations intact and zero accumulated fatigue. That's the entire game.

The science is clearer than most runners think. A meta-analysis of 27 studies published in Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise identified the optimal taper as progressive — not an instant cut — where volume decreases by 41–60% over roughly two weeks, while intensity and frequency remain unchanged. That last part is counterintuitive. You don't slow down during a taper. You run fewer miles per session, but you run them at race pace or threshold effort. Dropping intensity is the single fastest way to feel flat and underprepared on race day.

The Fuelling Side

For a 10k, traditional carbohydrate loading — the 48-hour high-carb protocol designed for marathons — is largely unnecessary. The race is short enough that your glycogen stores at normal carbohydrate intake (5–7g/kg/day) will be sufficient. The Science in Sport fuelling guide's recommendation for a 10k is more targeted: maintain consistent carbohydrate in the 2–3 days before, don't introduce new foods, and prioritise a solid pre-race meal 2–3 hours before the start (1–4g carbohydrate per kg bodyweight, low fibre, familiar).

During the race itself, a single gel or a few sips of sports drink at the 4–5km mark isn't physiologically necessary for most runners completing in under 60 minutes — but the research suggests a small amount of carbohydrate may have a direct effect on the central nervous system, reducing the perception of effort. Caffeine gel in the final kilometre is a legitimate performance tool if your gut tolerates it in training.

"The biggest mistake people make in tapering is that they don't trust it. Distance runners have to have discipline, but it's a different type of discipline to back off."

— Thom Hunt, former American 10K record holder, via Outside Online

The taper crazies — the itch, the anxiety, the feeling that two weeks of reduced running is erasing fitness you spent months building — are normal and almost universal. Research from Frontiers in Sport and Active Living (158,000+ runners) found that structured 3-week tapers were associated with finishing 2.6% faster than shorter, less structured tapers. The hay is in the barn. The next two weeks are about showing up with fresh legs, not adding to the stack.

Efteling

  • Hooghmoed — a brand-new free-fall tower attraction — opens May 1, 2026
  • Located right next to Baron 1898; theming matches the existing steampunk mine aesthetic
  • Capacity: 600 guests/hour across three lift towers

    • The park announced Hooghmoed ("Arrogance" in Dutch — it's a word with seven deadly sins theming) back in spring 2025, and it opens in six weeks. The free-fall ride sits beside the park's beloved dive coaster, Baron 1898, which means the Mine area is becoming one of the denser experience clusters in the park. The attraction is designed for families — shorter than a dedicated thrill drop tower, high throughput, and in keeping with Efteling's signature folklore theming. A raven-flight concept ride is also in development for later in 2026. Efteling.com →

    Did You Know? Efteling's "Sprookjesbos" (Fairytale Forest) — the oldest section of the park — opened in 1952 and is still one of the most emotionally resonant areas of any European theme park. Several of its original fairy tale tableaux are now over 70 years old.

    Three days of music, comedy, art, and a dedicated family programme at Ballymully Cottage Farm, Limavady. One of the most genuinely family-friendly festivals on the island. Day tickets and weekend camping available. Worth booking early — it sells out.

    Marble Arch Caves — any weekend

    Genuinely underrated. A 1.5km guided walk through underground rivers, waterfalls, and huge chambers in Fermanagh — including a short boat section (water levels permitting). Not a tourist trap: it's spectacular. Book in advance.

    Montalto Estate Gardens, Co. Down

    Multiple themed gardens (Alpine, Winter, Formal), a natural children's play area, award-winning café, and shop. Gardens peak through May–June. Something of a hidden gem even for locals — very few queues, genuinely beautiful.
