Open. Closed. Open Again. Nobody Knows.

The Strait of Hormuz — the world's most important 33 nautical miles. Wikimedia Commons / public domain.
The strait is only 33 nautical miles wide at its narrowest point. Two inbound and two outbound shipping lanes, each 2 miles wide, separated by a 2-mile buffer zone.
  • Iran declared Hormuz "strictly controlled" on Saturday — less than 48 hours after the foreign minister called it "completely open"
  • Iran's IRGC says the closure holds until the US lifts its blockade of Iranian ports — a condition Washington has not agreed to
  • A Lebanon ceasefire began Friday at midnight; Israel's military reported "multiple violations" within hours

    • The most important 33 nautical miles of water on the planet have spent this week in a state of deliberate ambiguity. On Friday, Iran's Foreign Minister Saeed Khatibzadeh told reporters that the Strait of Hormuz was "completely open," a statement Donald Trump immediately seized on as a breakthrough. By Saturday morning, Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps issued a contradictory declaration: the strait would remain "under strict control" until the United States lifted its blockade on Iranian port traffic.

    Why It Matters
    • ~20% of the world's oil passes through Hormuz daily
    • ~35% of global LNG exports
    • UK heating oil prices already surging — NI worst affected
    • UK Gov: £50m emergency heating oil support package

    For shipping firms, this ambiguity is operationally paralyzing. Whether an insurer considers the strait "safe" has enormous consequences for route planning and premium costs. Several major carriers have been diverting vessels around the Cape of Good Hope — adding roughly two weeks to journey times — rather than risk the passage. The divergence between what Iran's diplomatic and military wings are saying reflects a deeper tension within Tehran about how far to escalate before the domestic economic cost becomes unbearable.

    The week's other significant development was the Lebanon ceasefire, which took effect at midnight local time on Friday. Fireworks went off over Beirut — followed, within hours, by Lebanese military reports of Israeli "acts of aggression" violating the terms. A 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah was supposed to give diplomats space to negotiate; the early signals suggest that space is going to be contested. Iran-backed Hezbollah's strategic position is now deeply intertwined with the main conflict, and any diplomatic track with Tehran will need to account for that entanglement.

    Trump, for his part, has been characteristically bullish. "We're on the verge of victory very soon," he told a rally on Thursday, adding that the war has only lasted "two months" — a framing that conveniently ignores the protracted nature of sanctions, proxy conflict, and the humanitarian consequences accumulating across the region. US military officials simultaneously briefed Congress that Iran still possesses "thousands of missiles and drones" despite the significant degradation of launch infrastructure. The gap between the President's public confidence and the intelligence community's private assessment is, by now, a structural feature of this conflict.

    The Angle

    The Hormuz ambiguity is not a diplomatic accident — it's a deliberate Iranian information strategy. By allowing two contradictory signals to coexist, Tehran gets to observe how markets and shipping firms respond without fully committing to either position. It's a pressure instrument that costs nothing to deploy. The real question is whether Washington understands this well enough to resist responding to the statement that suits its preferred narrative.

    What Actually Sells on Etsy in 2026

  • Digital downloads are Etsy's highest-margin category — zero inventory, zero shipping, automatic delivery
  • Bundles consistently outperform single listings at every price point
  • Notion templates are growing; Kindle Scribe templates are under-served and worth watching

    • The Etsy digital products market has matured considerably since the pandemic boom, and the picture in 2026 is clearer than it's ever been: what actually sells is categorically different from what gets talked about in creator YouTube circles.

    The Hierarchy in Practice

    The highest-velocity categories are PNG clipart sets, SVG cut files, and tumbler/mug wraps — design files that crafters, Cricut users, and sublimation printers buy repeatedly and in bulk. A 50-file SVG bundle at £8 outperforms a single file at £2 in every metric: higher average order value, better Etsy algorithm signal, and more repeat customers.

    Notion templates sit in a smaller but growing niche. The top creators — Thomas Frank, Easlo — have earned extraordinary sums, but the market has stratified. Generic productivity templates face steep competition. Niche wins: academic research dashboards, fitness tracking systems, creator workflow tools. The sweet spot is £8–£25 for a template that solves a specific, verifiable problem.

    The Kindle Scribe template market is genuinely under-served. Most analysis tools show low seller counts (roughly 1,300 active sellers) against steady search demand. Productivity bundles, journal templates, and planning systems for the device are converting well — and the device's user base is growing as Amazon continues pushing the Scribe ecosystem.

    Market Signals — April 2026
    • Notion templates: £500–£5,000/month realistic for niche creators with 50+ listings
    • Bundle strategy: sell 20-file packs at £5–£12 rather than single files at £1–2
    • Kindle Scribe: ~1,300 sellers, strong demand — low competition window
    • SEO tools: Everbee and EtsyHunt for listing-level data before entering a niche
    • Listing fee: £0.20 + 6.5% transaction on Etsy — price accordingly

    "I made £3,000 in my first month selling templates. Most sales came while I was sleeping, from customers in different time zones."

    — r/Notion user, quoted via EzyCourse

    The Scaling Question

    The bottleneck for most template sellers isn't design quality — it's listing volume. One seller in the MyDesigns community went from 12 listings to 400+ in a month by batch-creating design sets; the algorithm rewarded the activity with better visibility. For Notion and Kindle Scribe templates specifically, the approach is: identify an under-served niche via search volume data, build 5–10 variations of a core template, price at the mid-to-high range, and invest in clean mockup photography. The mockup is often the difference between clicking and scrolling past.

    Atlético Madrid vs Arsenal

    First leg: 29 April, Wanda Metropolitano
    Second leg: 5 May, Emirates Stadium

    Head versus gut. Arsenal lead the Premier League, have conceded five goals in twelve CL matches, and are statistically the most consistent team in Europe. Atletico, however, have beaten Barcelona twice in elimination football this season. Simeone was born to win a match like this.

    The quarter-final that deserves a moment of its own: Bayern 4–3 Real Madrid in Munich, 6–4 on aggregate, was a chaotic masterpiece. A Güler free kick after 35 seconds, a Mbappe breakaway, a Camavinga red card in the 86th minute, then goals from Díaz and Olise on the final kick. It was, by any measure, an instant classic — Bayern knocked out the 15-time champions in stunning fashion and now must do it again against PSG in the semis.

    The Taper Paradox: How Doing Less Makes You Faster

    Two weeks before a 10k is a strange place to be. You've done the hard work — the interval sessions, the long easy runs, the strength work that was supposed to make your legs more resilient. And now the plan says to do less of it. Significantly less. The body's instinct is to rebel. The mind is convinced that one more hard session will make the difference. The science disagrees.

    What the Research Actually Shows

    A meta-analysis of 27 studies published in Medicine & Science in Sports & Exercise found that the tapering strategy most likely to produce significant performance improvements is a two-week window in which training volume is progressively decreased by 41–60% — without reducing training intensity or frequency. That's the key. You run fewer miles per session, but you keep the pace of your quality sessions identical. You don't stop lifting; you reduce sets and load while keeping effort the same.

    The reason this works is physiological: during a heavy training block, you're in a constant state of functional overreaching. Muscle glycogen is lower than optimal, connective tissue is accumulating micro-stress, and the nervous system is running slightly suppressed. The taper is when the body cashes in the fitness gains that hard training generated but couldn't express because of accumulated fatigue.

    The Numbers
    • Volume reduction: 41–60% from peak
    • Intensity: keep race-pace work in
    • Frequency: maintain session count
    • Duration: 7–14 days for 10k
    • Performance gain: 2–5% typically
    • Strength work: stop heavy leg work 5–7 days before race

    Strength Training in the Taper Window

    For runners who also lift — and particularly those running concurrent training blocks — the taper question becomes more complex. The evidence from r/AdvancedRunning and from coaches like those at Strength for Endurance broadly supports the same principle: maintain intensity, reduce volume. Keep the same weight on the bar but drop sets. Upper body and core work can continue until two or three days before race day. Heavy leg work — squats, Romanian deadlifts, loaded carries — should stop five to seven days out. The goal is to eliminate any risk of DOMS arriving on race morning.

    The counterintuitive finding from several studies: athletes who cut both intensity and volume during the taper often report feeling "flat" on race day. The sharpening effect — that coiled-spring sensation — requires some stimulus. A Tuesday sharpening session of 3–4 miles with four 400m repeats at goal race pace is the classic approach: enough neuromuscular priming to feel race-ready, not enough to generate meaningful fatigue. Then: Thursday mobility, Friday shakeout run (15–20 minutes + four strides), Saturday race.

    The psychology of the taper is its own challenge. Feeling rested can feel like falling behind. Energy levels spike as fatigue clears, which can be misread as a signal to train more. The phrase "hay is in the barn" is clichéd for a reason: no meaningful fitness gains are made in the final six days. What's gained is freshness, glycogen supercompensation, and nervous system recovery. The temptation to do one more long run the weekend before a race is one of the most common sources of suboptimal race performances at every level.

    Did You Know? The taper length that optimises performance varies by race distance: 7–10 days for a 10k, 11–14 days for a 15k, 19–22 days for a full marathon (per Mayo Clinic Health System). Tapering too long for a short race can produce the same flat feeling as not tapering at all.

    Rates, ISAs, and the War Premium

    Coming into 2026, markets expected the Bank of England to cut rates through the year. What happened to that forecast?

    The Iran war happened. Oil prices surged in February as Hormuz tensions escalated, inflation expectations revised upward, and the BoE — which had been on a cautious cutting path from 5.25% to 3.75% — found itself in an awkward position. Markets had priced in a series of further cuts through 2026; now, per Morningstar analysis published this week, they're pricing rates ending the year at around 4% — meaning a hike rather than continued cuts.

    For those with cash savings, the current environment is actually reasonable: the average easy-access cash ISA is paying 4.65%, well above the most recent inflation figure of 3%. But the cash ISA clock is ticking — this is the last full year where savers under 65 can put the full £20,000 into cash. From April 2027, that cash limit drops to £12,000, with the remainder only usable in stocks & shares or innovative finance ISAs. If you've been meaning to use your full allowance and have been partial to cash, 2026/27 is the last chance at the full amount.
