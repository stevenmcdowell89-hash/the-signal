The Ceasefire That Wasn't — and the Strait at the Centre of Everything

Map of the Strait of Hormuz — the 21-mile choke point through which roughly 20% of global oil supply passes. / Wikimedia Commons
  • Iran's foreign minister declared the Strait open on Friday following a 10-day Lebanon ceasefire
  • By Saturday evening, the IRGC had reversed that, firing on two Indian-flagged ships and declaring the waterway closed again
  • The ceasefire between the US and Iran expires Wednesday — and no date for the next round of talks has been set

The sequence of events over the past 48 hours illustrates why every development in this conflict needs to be read in at least two registers simultaneously. On Friday, oil prices dropped, markets breathed, and headlines declared a breakthrough. On Saturday night, Revolutionary Guard gunboats fired on a tanker, the IRGC issued a statement declaring all vessels attempting transit would be treated as hostile, and India's foreign ministry summoned Tehran's ambassador after two of its flagged ships came under fire.

The Numbers
  • 20% — Share of global oil that transits the Strait
  • ~3,000 — Iranian deaths since Feb 28
  • 2,300+ — Lebanese fatalities
  • 23 — US killed (service members)
  • $50bn — Estimated oil infrastructure losses in 50 days
  • Apr 23 — Ceasefire expiry date

What happened is less a reversal of policy and more a demonstration of who actually controls the Strait. Iran's Foreign Ministry and its new Supreme Leader Ayatollah Moja Khamenei had signalled openness to talks; the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, which operates the naval assets, clearly had different orders. The IRGC statement was unambiguous: the strait remains "under stringent management and oversight" and no vessel should attempt transit. That the IRGC moved unilaterally while diplomatic signals were still in the air tells you something important about the internal fault lines in Tehran right now.

Pakistan has been working as a mediator, and US–Iran talks have reportedly made progress on fifteen points including nuclear material transfers. But Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Saeed Khatibzadeh was explicit on Sunday: no date for the next round of direct discussions has been set, and Washington's "maximalist demands" remain the sticking point. Trump, for his part, has warned Iran not to "blackmail" the United States, and US Central Command reports that 23 ships have already returned to Iranian waters since Monday's blockade began.

The Angle

The timing matters. The Lebanon ceasefire — a genuine if fragile diplomatic achievement — created space for the Strait to reopen. Iran's decision to close it again, even partially, suggests Tehran is using the Strait not as leverage toward resolution but as a permanent instrument of coercion. An Iran that opens and closes the waterway on its own timetable, regardless of wider diplomatic context, is a fundamentally different adversary than one negotiating in good faith. The ceasefire expiry on Wednesday is the next hard deadline.

Pragmata: The Weird One That Works

What does it mean for a game to be genuinely ambitious in 2026, when most "ambitious" means "bigger open world"?
Pragmata — Hugh and Diana in the near-future lunar city. / Polygon · Capcom

Pragmata, released Thursday on all platforms including Switch 2, is Capcom's answer to that question — and it's a strange, satisfying one. The game casts you as Hugh, a contractor in a near-future Moon city, working alongside Diana, a mysterious girl in a white suit who can hack machines. The combat has you simultaneously managing both characters: Diana opens enemy armour, Hugh exploits it with an eclectic arsenal. It's the kind of system that sounds confusing on paper and clicks completely once you're in it.

Reviews have been strong across the board — Nintendo Life gave it 9/10, Nintendo World Report called it "excellent," and even the more technically cautious Vooks gave it a full recommendation despite frame rate caveats. The Switch 2 version runs at an unlocked 30-50fps (docked), which is notably lower than PS5 but higher than the Xbox Series S comparison Digital Foundry used. DLSS boosts an internal 540p image to 1080p in docked mode; the result is "arguably competitive" against PS5, according to DF, particularly in motion.

The more interesting note is that this is another Capcom day-one Switch 2 release — following Resident Evil 1-3 in April. It confirms something that was speculative six months ago: Capcom has committed to the platform in a way that goes beyond token ports.

The Long Game — Personal Finance

The Last Full ISA Year

Is the 2026/27 tax year worth treating differently from every other ISA year? Yes — and here's why it's the last chance at the full £20,000.

The new tax year opened on April 6, resetting ISA allowances to £20,000. That's unchanged from last year — but it's also the last time under-65s will see that figure in a cash ISA. From April 2027, the Chancellor's Autumn Budget 2025 decision kicks in: the cash ISA limit for under-65s drops to £12,000. Stocks and Shares ISAs retain the full £20,000 cap. The message is clear — government policy is nudging savers away from cash and toward equity investing.

The State Pension Rise

The Triple Lock delivered a 4.8% uplift from April 6 — the largest earnings-linked rise in several years. The full new state pension is now £241.30/week, up from £230.25. For 12 million pensioners that's approximately £575 more per year. The complication: with the personal tax-free allowance frozen until 2031 at £12,570, the annual state pension (£12,548) is now within touching distance of the threshold. A further rise next year could push some pensioners into paying income tax on their pension alone for the first time.

The Niche Stack: How Template Sellers Are Winning in 2026

£0
to £500/month isn't about working harder — research consistently shows it comes down to one strategic shift.

The digital template market on Etsy is not shrinking — it's sorting. The sellers making consistent passive income in 2026 share one characteristic: they niched down past the obvious. Not "Notion template." Not even "Notion planner template." Something like "Notion CRM for freelance photographers" or "Kindle Scribe weekly journal for strength athletes." The specificity sounds limiting. In practice it means almost zero competition, higher buyer intent, and reviews that write themselves because the product actually fits the buyer's exact use case.

What's Moving Right Now

According to eRank data and community reports from r/Etsy, the highest-converting digital categories on Etsy in early 2026 are:

  • Notion template bundles — Life planners, student study dashboards, and freelancer CRMs in the £10-30 range. Bundles (buying 3 templates together) consistently outperform single listings in the algorithm.
  • Kindle Scribe interactive PDFs — Planner, journal, and goal-tracker formats. The device's native annotation makes these high-utility. The market is less crowded than Notion and buyers are specifically searching.
  • PNG/SVG cut file bundles — Less obviously relevant, but huge volume. Design file bundles at £5-12 for 20+ files drive repeat purchases from print-on-demand sellers.
  • Printable party kits — Every birthday, baby shower, and graduation is a buying event. Niche by theme (dinosaurs, specific sports teams) rather than "generic birthday."
The EverBee Method
  • Search for your template idea on Etsy
  • Use EverBee to analyse competitor shops — monthly revenue, top products
  • Find the sub-niche with real sales volume but fewer than 50 competing listings
  • That's your gap. Build 3 variants around it before launching
  • Price at £8-20 for standalone; £20-35 for bundles
Did you know? The digital products that drive the most repeat purchases on Etsy are design files (PNG/SVG), not planners — because Cricut and print-on-demand sellers need fresh designs constantly and buy in batches.

The Notion Seller's Advantage in 2026

Building Notion templates to sell is conceptually different from using Notion personally — and it's worth treating them as separate disciplines. The templates that sell are purpose-built for a specific type of buyer (not a general planner), they have professional cover images designed in Canva, and they're listed with keyword-optimised titles that match buyer search language. "Aesthetic Notion Life Planner" converts significantly better than "Notion Template Pack." The positioning is the product, not just the database.

Star Wars: Maul – Shadow Lord. Chapters 5 and 6 drop Monday morning. / Lucasfilm · Disney+

The Two-Week Window

"Everything is done. The work is banked. The only thing you can do now is arrive fresh."

— The core principle of pre-race taper, consistent across Barbell Medicine, Faster Road Racing, and Team RunRun's 10k guides

Two weeks before a 10k race is a specific window with specific rules — and the instinct most runners feel (to do more, to squeeze in one last hard session, to "stay sharp") is exactly wrong. The research on taper duration for a 10k is unusually consistent: 7-10 days of reduced volume with maintained intensity is the sweet spot. The goal isn't rest. It's arriving at the start line with full glycogen stores, fresh legs, and no accumulated fatigue from the previous four weeks.

Here's what that looks like in practice. Two weeks out: keep the same number of sessions, cut total mileage by 20-25%. This week's long run should be your last full-distance effort at around 80-85% effort — not all-out, but enough to confirm the pace is there. Strength work continues at normal frequency but with volume reduced slightly; the last thing you want is delayed-onset muscle soreness going into race week. Next week: mileage drops further to 40-50% of peak. Three days before the race is the last meaningful run — short intervals of 100-200m at race pace or faster, to keep the neuromuscular system primed without adding fatigue. Two days out: optional 10-minute easy shake-out. Race eve: nothing.

On the Interference Question

If you're still lifting during race week — and there's no reason you shouldn't be, the research is clear that concurrent training doesn't compromise either adaptation when recovery is adequate — the practical rule is to avoid heavy lower body work in the four days before the race. Upper body and push work is fine. The concern isn't mTOR vs AMPK molecular conflict (that's been largely debunked by three recent meta-analyses); it's simply that leg DOMS on race day is the practical problem to avoid. Barbell Medicine's overview of the interference effect is the most evidence-based single piece on this topic.

Did you know? A 2025 meta-analysis showed that moderate-intensity continuous training (running at lactate threshold or below) produces essentially zero interference with hypertrophy when separated by at least 6 hours from resistance sessions — the classic "two-a-day" concern turns out to apply mainly to high-intensity cardio.

Efteling Adds Its First New Ride of 2026

Hooghmoed opens May 1. The free-fall towers sit directly next to Baron 1898, sharing its industrial mine theming. / Efteling

Hooghmoed opens May 1 — just six weeks away. Efteling's new free-fall attraction sits directly next to Baron 1898 and shares its mining/chimney theme: visitors play the role of young chimney sweeps tasked with ascending the Baron's gold smeltery to clean it. Three 12-metre towers, a 6-metre free fall, rotating platforms with views over the mine area, and swirling mist effects. The capacity is 600 guests/hour across three towers of ten per ride.

The key practical detail: minimum height is 90cm (not 130cm like Baron 1898 itself), which means this is specifically designed for smaller riders who can't yet tackle the main coaster. Children from 90cm can ride alone if accompanied by someone 130cm+ and aged 16+. Tickets don't require separate booking — it's included in standard park admission.

Did you know? Hooghmoed is named after the Dutch word for hubris or arrogance — fitting for a character like the Baron who overreached. The attraction is part of Efteling's "area vision" to keep Baron 1898's surrounding zone thematically complete.
Hooghmoed Quick Specs
  • Opens — 1 May 2026

  • Type — Free-fall drop tower (×3)
  • Height — 12m towers, 6m free fall
  • Min height — 90cm
  • Capacity — 600/hour
  • Booking — Standard admission, no extra cost

The context for the June/July trip: this means anyone visiting Efteling from June 30 onward gets Hooghmoed as part of the experience. It's a meaningful addition to what was already the strongest area of the park — the Baron zone tends to have some of the best queue-management and theming quality. The ride fills a gap that previously forced smaller visitors to skip the area entirely.

Other Efteling news for 2026: a new show and attraction for the Raveleijn theme area is planned for the end of the year, plus Efteling's 31st Fairytale ("The Princess and the Pea") is also in development. The Grand Hotel continues to be well-reviewed by families for its in-park location overlooking the Aquanura water show.
