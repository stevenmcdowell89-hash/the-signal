Efteling — The Fairy-Tale Kingdom

Older Than Disneyland, and Twice the Size

The Princess and the Pea pavilion in the Fairytale Forest — Efteling's 31st fairy tale, opened May 2025. Efteling Press

The story of Efteling begins not with fairy tales but with football. In 1933, two clergymen in the small North Brabant town of Kaatsheuvel proposed building a sports park. By 1950, the Efteling Nature Park Foundation had been established with a more ambitious goal: attract tourists. The mayor's wife, Betty Perquin, proposed the idea that changed everything — a garden of real fairy tales, brought to life in the forest.

To make it happen, they recruited Anton Pieck — already one of the Netherlands' most celebrated illustrators, known for his atmospheric editions of the Brothers Grimm. Pieck created approximately 1,500 sketches and drawings for the Fairytale Forest while filmmaker Peter Reijnders engineered the mechanical effects. Pieck's one non-negotiable rule: everything must look like it had been there for a hundred years. Stone walls, real roof tiles, no cheap plastic. That philosophy has defined the park ever since. (Efteling)

On 31 May 1952 — three years before Disneyland opened in California — the Fairytale Forest opened to the public. Entry cost 80 Dutch cents. The first year drew 222,941 visitors. Today, Efteling covers 180 acres (twice the original Disneyland), welcomes over 5 million visitors annually, and 94% of the Dutch population has been at least once. It is owned not by a corporation but by a non-profit foundation. (Wikipedia)

When Anton Pieck died in November 1987 at the age of 92, an unfinished drawing was still attached to his drawing board. He never stopped working. The park's colour palette today is still based on his original specifications. (Efteling Designers)

The Fairytale Forest

Thirty-One Stories in Fifteen Acres of Woodland

Little Red Riding Hood in the Sprookjesbos. Efteling

The heart of Efteling is the Sprookjesbos — 15 acres of ancient woodland containing 31 fairy-tale dioramas. Every scene uses animatronics, sound design, and obsessive craftsmanship to bring the stories to life. Ten scenes are from the Brothers Grimm (Snow White, Sleeping Beauty, Rapunzel, Hansel and Gretel, Rumpelstiltskin, and more), five from Hans Christian Andersen (including The Little Mermaid and The Chinese Nightingale), and three from Charles Perrault (Little Red Riding Hood, Tom Thumb, Cinderella). (Wikipedia)

Allow at least 90 minutes to explore properly. Look for hidden Easter eggs — secret squirrels, talking trees, and a hidden branch where the Chinese Nightingale once sat. The newest addition, The Princess and the Pea, opened in May 2025 as the 31st fairy tale.

The Rides

From Gentle Dark Rides to a 90 km/h Dive Coaster

Symbolica — Efteling's €35 million trackless dark ride, widely considered one of the finest in the world. Efteling

A 10-year-old at typical height (135–140 cm) can access almost every ride listed here. Efteling's genius is that its best attractions aren't just thrill rides — they're stories you sit inside.

Symbolica: Palace of Fantasy

2017 · €35 million · Trackless dark ride · No min. height with adult

Efteling's most expensive attraction ever. Three different routes through the magical palace of King Pardulf, each revealing different rooms and secrets. Use the virtual queue in the app or go first thing in the morning. Widely regarded as one of the finest dark rides on the planet.

✓ Ideal for 10-year-olds

Droomvlucht (Dreamflight)

1993 · Suspended dark ride · No min. height · All ages

Gondolas float Peter Pan-style above fairy worlds filled with fairies, castles, and trolls. The original score by Ruud Bos has been making people cry since 1993. Walk to the back of the park first thing — near-zero queues in the first 45 minutes.

✓ Universally loved

Danse Macabre

October 2024 · World's first Dynamic Motion Stage ride · Min. 120 cm

Brand new. Replaced the iconic Spookslot. A spinning, tilting, lifting ride synchronised to Saint-Saëns' Danse Macabre — themed around a ghostly orchestra and cursed conductor Joseph Charlatan. Won the 2025 Golden Ticket Award for Best New Family Attraction. Dark and intense, but brilliant. (Efteling)

✓ Ages 8+ — may be spooky for sensitive kids

Baron 1898

2015 · B&M dive coaster · Min. 132 cm · 90 km/h

Efteling's most intense ride. Near-vertical 37.5-metre drop into a mine shaft with an agonising hang over the edge before release. The preshow — featuring the ghostly Witte Wieven projection — is considered the best on any dive coaster in the world. Currently closed for maintenance until April 30, 2026, but will be fully operational for a late June visit.

Depends on height & nerve

De Vliegende Hollander (The Flying Dutchman)

2007 · Water coaster + dark ride · Min. 120 cm

Half dark ride, half water coaster. Sail through a 17th-century haunted port in atmospheric fog, then launch into an outdoor splash finale based on the Dutch legend of the cursed ghost ship. You will get somewhat wet. (Coaster101)

✓ Ideal for 10-year-olds

Hooghmoed — NEW for 2026

Opens May 1, 2026 · Drop tower · Min. 90 cm with adult

Brand-new junior drop tower built next to Baron 1898, sharing the same mining-smeltery theme. Three 12-metre towers, 6-metre free fall with spinning rotation, mist and smoke effects. Designed specifically for visitors who can't yet ride Baron 1898. Will be open well before your July visit. (Efteling)

✓ Perfect for 10-year-olds

Joris en de Draak (George and the Dragon)

2010 · Double-track wooden racing coaster · Min. 110 cm with adult

Two simultaneous tracks — Fire and Water — race each other, with a photo-finish winner declared at the end. Choose your dragon. Single rider queue available. One of the most popular rides in the park.

✓ Ideal for 10-year-olds

Other family highlights include Fata Morgana (a boat ride through the world of 1001 Nights with 140 animatronics — often compared to Pirates of the Caribbean), Vogel Rok (fast indoor roller coaster in near-complete darkness), Villa Volta (a Madhouse illusion ride), Max & Moritz (twin family coasters), and Piraña (river rapids — expect to get soaked). The Gondoletta is a peaceful 20-minute boat cruise around the park lake — the perfect break between adrenaline.

Did You Know?

The Gondoletta was never meant to be permanent. It was built as a prototype water transport system for a 1001 Nights dark ride that was never built. The prototype became one of the park's most beloved attractions — still running 40+ years later. (Coasterpedia)

Shows & Events

What's On During Your Visit

Aquanura — the largest water show in Europe — runs multiple times daily. Hundreds of fountains choreographed to music, fire, and lights. Spectacular at dusk. Free to watch, no booking needed.

Magicaluna — a brand-new 20-minute daytime illusion show debuting April 3, 2026 at the Efteling Theatre. Non-verbal, about a teenage girl whose dream builders transform her bedroom into a spectacle. Runs daily at 12:30, 13:30, and 14:30 through November 2026. (Theme Park Insider)

Summer Efteling (July and August) brings extended opening hours — until 8pm most nights, and until 11pm on Fridays and Saturdays. Extra characters, entertainment, food, and a Summer Beach play area. (Efteling Summer Season)

Note: The Raveleijn outdoor arena show is closed through 2026 for a complete overhaul (new show, new ride, new food court — reopening end of 2026). You won't see it this trip, but expect something spectacular next time. (Magic Breaks)

The Design Philosophy

Why Efteling Feels Different

Visitors from Disney and Universal parks are often struck by something they can't quite articulate about Efteling. It comes down to five principles that have held since 1952:

Handcrafted authenticity. Every building uses real stone, carved wood, painted murals, and hand-placed detail. The absence of corporate design efficiency is palpable. (Vocal Media)

Nature integration. The park was built within existing woodland. Trees, ponds, moss, and natural growth are woven into every attraction. Efteling feels like a forest that happens to contain wonders.

Storytelling primacy. Every space tells a story — from the grandest ride to the humblest bin. The famous Holle Bolle Gijs paper-eating bins have been crying "Papier hier!" since 1959. Even they have a backstory and original sound design.

Non-corporate ownership. Because Efteling is a non-profit foundation, design decisions serve creative and cultural values, not shareholder returns. Attractions aren't bulldozed for being unprofitable — they're maintained and honoured.

The Take

Efteling is what happens when you build a theme park on artistic integrity instead of IP licensing. Anton Pieck and Ton van de Ven — the two visionaries who shaped the park across five decades — believed rides should be stories you inhabit, not brands you recognise. The result is a park that feels handmade in a way that no Disney park, however brilliant, can replicate.

Accommodation — Nights 1 & 2

The Wonder Hotel

The Efteling Wonder Hotel — your fairy-tale castle base for two nights. Efteling

Originally opened in 1992 as the Efteling Hotel, fully renovated and relaunched as the Wonder Hotel in April 2025. Designed as a "castle in the clouds" with four distinctive towers, 124 rooms and suites, and subtle fairy-tale references hidden throughout — mice in the walls, Red Shoes in the cupboard, story Easter eggs in the room design. (Efteling Press)

What's Included With Your Stay

30-Minute Early Entry

Access the park 30 minutes before public opening via a private hotel entrance. This is the single most valuable perk — reach Symbolica or Droomvlucht with near-zero queues before the masses arrive.

Unlimited Park Access

Your park tickets cover all nights of stay, including arrival and departure days. With a June 30 check-in and July 2 check-out, that's three full days in Efteling.

Also included: breakfast buffet daily (7:30–10:30 am, with character visits from Efteling characters), free parking, and a private hotel entrance to the park — no queuing at the main gate. (Efteling Wonder Hotel)

Dining at the Hotel

Restaurant Saga is the hotel's main sit-down dinner restaurant — themed, highly rated, and worth booking in advance. De Gelagkamer hotel bar serves snacks and cocktails into the evening, with games available. The breakfast restaurant offers a themed buffet that, according to consistent guest feedback, is made particularly special for children by character visits. (TripAdvisor)

Did You Know?

Since August 2025, Efteling also has the Grand Hotel — the first hotel located inside the park itself, with views of the Aquanura water show and the Fairytale Forest. It set a Guinness World Record on opening day for the world's largest ribbon bow (16.74m × 17.15m). You're at the Wonder Hotel this trip — but note it for next time. (Efteling Press)

Beekse Bergen — The Wild Side

The Largest Wildlife Safari in the Benelux

The car safari at Beekse Bergen — where giraffes lean in to inspect your vehicle. Beekse Bergen

Twenty minutes from Efteling, in the Noord-Brabant countryside between Tilburg and Hilvarenbeek, sits a 120-hectare open savannah housing approximately 1,250 animals from over 150 species. When Safaripark Beekse Bergen opened in 1968 as the "Lion Park," it was the first safari park in the Netherlands — a revolutionary concept where visitors could observe lions from their own car with no fence between them. (Wikipedia)

In 2024, the park broke its all-time record with 1.5 million visitors. Its parent company, Libéma, acquired Paignton Zoo in the UK in January 2026 and has announced a ten-year expansion plan. This is not a roadside petting zoo — it's one of Europe's most important conservation centres, coordinating the European Endangered Species Programme (EEP) for both Southern white rhinoceros and Southern African cheetah. (Beekse Bergen)

Four Ways to Safari

All Included With One Ticket

All four safari experiences are included in a single entrance ticket — no surcharges. (Beekse Bergen)

🚗 Car Safari (Autosafari)

30–60 minutes · Drive your own vehicle · No fence between you and the animals

The signature experience. Lions, leopards, zebras, giraffes, Cape buffalo, cheetahs, rhinos, wildebeest — all on open savannah as you drive through. Download the Beekse Bergen app beforehand for audio commentary synced to your route. The car safari closes 30 minutes before the rest of the park — go first thing. A giraffe stretching its neck into your car window is a genuine possibility. (Beekse Bergen)

🚶 Walking Safari

Self-paced · Lions, hyenas, elephants, penguins, giraffes · Adventure routes for kids

Set your own pace across the park on foot. The route passes through Elephant Valley — where visitors walk beneath a corridor connecting two elephant plains — and includes playground breaks built into the route. Perfect for active 10-year-olds. Wear comfortable shoes. (Beekse Bergen)

⛵ Boat Safari

30 minutes · Ranger-narrated · Rhinos, cheetahs, yaks

A scenic cruise aboard the Livingstone or Stanley, narrated by an experienced ranger. Spot animals you'd miss from the car — rhinos grazing on the banks, cheetahs hiding in undergrowth. The most relaxing safari — ideal after a busy morning on foot. Check departure times in the app. (Beekse Bergen)

🚌 Bus Safari

45 minutes · Guided by ranger · Props, bones, and droppings

An educational guided tour in two legs with a ranger on board. The interactive format is brilliant for kids — rangers bring animal furs, bones, and yes, droppings, for a sensory experience. Route 1 covers zebras, camels, rhinos, cheetahs. Route 2: giraffes, Cape buffalo, lions. (Beekse Bergen)

The Stars of the Show

Who You'll Meet

Lions — visible on walking, car, and bus safaris. The Simbara lion viewpoint is a must-see, especially at feeding time (check schedule on arrival). From Safari Resort cabins, some guests watch lion feeding from their own window.

Giraffes — the car safari's most iconic moment. In January 2025, four Nubian giraffe calves were born: males Bata, Kito, and Iggy, and female Milele — a remarkable breeding success for an IUCN-vulnerable species. By your July visit, they'll be 18 months old and visible on the open plains. (YouTube)

Cheetahs — Beekse Bergen coordinates the European cheetah breeding programme. In May 2025, five cheetah cubs were born — only the third litter born anywhere in Europe that year. Only around 7,000 cheetahs remain in the wild globally. (Beekse Bergen)

African Elephants — Elephant Valley is an architectural highlight. Three baby elephants — Mosi, Ajabu, and Tendai — prompted a 2-hectare expansion in 2023. The valley features a walking corridor underneath the elephants. The park's star is Calimero — Europe's largest elephant at over 3 tonnes. (Beekse Bergen)

White Rhinos — Beekse Bergen also coordinates the EEP for Southern white rhinos. The park's conservation chief, Lars Versteege, chairs the global Rhinoceros Taxon Advisory Group protecting all five rhino species worldwide.

Also look for: hippos (the feeding session is a visitor favourite), African penguins, spotted hyenas, leopards, African wild dogs, red pandas, Siberian tigers, okapis, and Przewalski's horses — a species once extinct in the wild, now being reintroduced with support from Beekse Bergen. (Beekse Bergen Conservation)

Did You Know?

Every giraffe's spot pattern is unique — just like a human fingerprint. Keepers at Beekse Bergen identify each giraffe by sight from their pattern alone. (Beekse Bergen)

Conservation

Not Just a Zoo — A Conservation Hub

Beekse Bergen raised €60,000 to build a wildlife corridor in Kenya — a strip of protected land connecting two nature reserves so elephants can roam safely without conflicting with farming communities. The Northern Corridor officially opened in August 2025, with Beekse Bergen's head elephant keeper and conservation director present at the ceremony. (Northern Corridors Project)

No animals at the park are taken from the wild. Every animal was either born here or arrived from another zoo through managed breeding programmes. The park participates in over a dozen European Endangered Species Programmes, with cheetah and white rhino as its flagships. (Beekse Bergen Conservation)

The Take

Beekse Bergen isn't behind glass. It's a park where you drive your own car through open savannah and a giraffe sticks its head through your window. The conservation work is genuine — this isn't theatre. The cheetah and rhino breeding programmes are among Europe's most important, and the Kenya wildlife corridor is real. Your visit directly funds that work.

Safari Hotel & Speelland

Waking up to giraffes from your room at the Safari Hotel. Beekse Bergen

The Safari Hotel Beekse Bergen is an African lodge-style hotel set directly on the park's Wongo savannahs. From your room window, you watch zebras, giraffes, and rhinos move past at dawn. The aesthetic is warm earth tones, textured fabrics, and African-inspired artwork — "upmarket game lodge" rather than gimmick. The hotel carries a 9.8/10 rating on Expedia. (Beekse Bergen Safari Hotel)

What's Included

Unlimited Attractions Pass

Unlimited access to Safaripark, Speelland water park, plus Eindhoven Zoo, ZooParc Overloon, and Aviodrome — brilliant for bad-weather days or variety.

Exclusive Experiences

Priority booking for the Gamedrive (open-top Land Cruiser, ranger-led, close-range encounters). Private Gamedrive option for your party only. Walk-with-a-ranger experience.

Dining at the Safari Hotel

Amma — breakfast and dinner buffet with views directly over the savannah. Breakfast is included with your stay. Nommos — à la carte dinner for a culinary evening with wildlife views. Hogon Bar & Lounge — casual all-day coffee, lunch, cocktails, and mocktails. At the nearby Safari Resort, Restaurant Moto serves grilled food with an African-inspired menu — and drinks at the bowling lane arrive by robot. (Beekse Bergen)

Speelland — The Water Park & Playground

Speelland is Beekse Bergen's dedicated fun zone — included in your Attractions Pass. The outdoor water park on Lake Victoria features the Waverider (speedboat carousel), Duo Water Slide, Aquashuttle, Adventure Island, flamingo-shaped pedal boats, water gun battles, and a sandy beach with swimming. At 10 years old and typical height, your son has access to nearly every attraction.

Speelland Indoor — an all-weather adventure island with climbing frames, slides, ball pools, and trampolines — is open year-round. A lifesaver for the inevitable rainy Netherlands afternoon. (Speelland)

Belfast to Brabant — and Getting Around

Amsterdam — your gateway to Noord-Brabant. Unsplash

Schiphol → Efteling

Three Options, One Recommendation

🚗 Rental Car (Recommended)

~1h 15 min · €30–70/day · Maximum flexibility for the full trip

The strongest option for this trip. All major rental companies are at Schiphol Plaza. Budget €350–500 for a 7-day rental with fuel. Gives total freedom for the Efteling-to-Beekse-Bergen transfer (23 minutes), day trips to Den Bosch and Tilburg, and stress-free luggage handling with a child. Book in advance for best rates.

🚆 Train + Bus

~2h 15 min · €21–30 per person · Comfortable, frequent

Intercity train from Schiphol Airport Station to 's-Hertogenbosch (Den Bosch) (~68 min, 78 trains/day), then Bus 300 or 301 to Efteling (~20 min, every 20 min). The bus stops at the park entrance. Book via NS (Dutch Railways) or just tap your contactless bank card on board. (NS)

🚌 FlixBus Direct

~1h 50 min · €9–14 per person · Once daily only

Direct service from Amsterdam Bijlmer Arena (not Schiphol) to Efteling. Cheapest option, but only one departure per day (8:45am from Bijlmer), making it inflexible. Book at flixbus.com.

Efteling → Beekse Bergen

Twenty Minutes Apart

The two parks are remarkably close — just 22 km apart, a 23-minute drive via the N261 and N65. With a rental car, this is trivially easy. Without one, pre-book a local taxi (€70–85) or take Bus 301 to Tilburg then Bus 142/143 to Hilvarenbeek Safaripark (~1h 15 min). (Rome2Rio)

Dutch Travel Essentials

Apps, Payment, Plugs, Language

Payment: Contactless debit/credit card is accepted almost everywhere and is the default. Apple Pay and Google Pay work widely. Cash (euros) is accepted most places but some businesses are card-only. Avoid large notes (€100/€200). Your Irish bank card works via contactless. (Holland.com)

Apps to download: 9292 (the definitive Dutch public transport planner), NS (train tickets), Google Maps (offline maps for Noord-Brabant), Efteling app, Beekse Bergen 2.0 app. (Ubigi)

Power plugs: The Netherlands uses Type C/F (round two-pin). Irish/UK plugs will not fit without a European adapter. Buy one before departure. Voltage is 230V/50Hz — same as Ireland, so no converter needed. (Electrical Safety First)

Language: English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas and among younger people. Staff at both parks speak excellent English. That said, a few words of Dutch are always appreciated: Dank je wel (thank you), Alsjeblieft (please/here you go), and Lekker! (delicious). (Expat Explore)

Tap water: Among the best in Europe and completely safe to drink. Fill a reusable bottle throughout the trip. Not chlorinated. (Wise)

Tipping: Refreshingly relaxed. Service workers receive fair wages. 5–10% at restaurants for good service. Round up at bars. No guilt. (DutchReview)

Did You Know?

You can now tap any contactless bank card directly on Dutch buses, trains, and trams — no special transit card needed. The system is called OVpay and it means tourists no longer need to buy a separate OV-chipkaart. Your Irish Visa or Mastercard just works. (Holland.com)

Weather

What to Expect in Late June / Early July

Think unpredictable British summer but slightly warmer and sunnier. Average highs of 19–23°C, lows of 12–15°C. June is the brightest month — sunrise around 5:15am, sunset after 9:45pm. Expect about 9 days of rain in June, typically short sharp showers rather than prolonged downpours. Pack for warm days with occasional showers and a couple of potentially hot days (28–30°C during a heat event). (WeatherSpark)

The Essential Dutch Food List

What to Eat and Where to Find It

Stroopwafel

Thin waffle sandwich with caramel syrup · Markets, bakeries, everywhere

The Netherlands' most famous export after bicycles and tulips. Buy them fresh from a market stall — the warm, gooey caramel version bears no resemblance to the packaged supermarket version you've tried at home.

Bitterballen

Crispy fried beef ragù balls with mustard · Any Dutch café or bar

The universal Dutch pub snack. Crispy breadcrumbed exterior, molten beef ragù interior, served with sharp Dutch mustard. Order a portion at any bar or café. Your son will destroy them.

Bossche Bol

Massive cream puff coated in dark chocolate · 's-Hertogenbosch

Noord-Brabant's signature treat. A cream puff the size of a small planet, entirely glazed in dark chocolate. Originates from Bakkerij Royal on Visstraat in Den Bosch — 25 minutes from Beekse Bergen. Non-negotiable day-trip food goal.

Bossche Bollen — reason enough for a day trip to Den Bosch. Toine's Kitchen

Kibbeling

Battered fried cod pieces with tartar sauce · Market stalls

Dutch fish and chips, but better. Crispy battered cod nuggets served in a cone with creamy tartar sauce. Find them at any market stall or fishing harbour.

Poffertjes

Tiny fluffy pancakes with butter and powdered sugar · Markets, cafés

Miniature Dutch pancakes dusted in powdered sugar and drowned in butter. Available at Efteling near the Steam Carousel at 't Poffertje, and at market stalls across Brabant.

Worstenbrood

Brabant sausage roll · Every baker in the province

The Brabant version of a sausage roll — soft white bread wrapped around spiced minced meat. Unlike anything British. On UNESCO's intangible heritage list since 2016. Available from every baker year-round. (VisitBrabant)

Also try: Dutch fries (friet) — thick-cut, twice-fried, served in a cone with mayo (or patatje oorlog: mayo + peanut sauce + onion). Hagelslag — chocolate sprinkles on buttered bread, yes, as a breakfast meal. Pannekoek — large thin Dutch pancakes. Appeltaart — deep Dutch apple cake. Kroket — breadcrumbed croquette from a FEBO vending machine. Drop — Dutch liquorice, an acquired taste.

Where to Eat

Near Both Parks

At Efteling: Polles Keuken — spectacular interior theming, famous Dutch pancakes (book weeks ahead). Het Witte Paard — excellent value self-service with healthy options. Bäckerei Krümel — sheet cake and bread pizzas near Max & Moritz. The park specialty is the Kurkentrekker — a spiral pastry with fillings like chocolate mousse and caramel. (Travelista)

Near Efteling (Kaatsheuvel): De Molen (top-rated, French and seafood), Bij Anton (authentic Dutch eetcafé on Anton Pieckplein), De Roestelberg (hidden gem near the National Park). (TripAdvisor)

Near Beekse Bergen (Hilvarenbeek): Roots (local produce, modern Dutch cooking). Worth a day trip: Tilburg (15 min) for Piushaven canalside dining. 's-Hertogenbosch (25 min) for the Bossche Bol and medieval city centre.

Brabant Specialities

Regional Treasures

Trappist beer: Noord-Brabant has two of the world's nine Authentic Trappist breweries — De Koningshoeven (La Trappe) in Berkel-Enschot (near Tilburg, well worth a visit) and De Kievit in Zundert. Monk-brewed excellence.

Schrobbelèr: Local Tilburg herb liqueur made from 43 herbs and spices. Try it as a digestif at a local bar.

Duingoed Kaas: Prize-winning cheese from the Loon and Drunen Dunes area, right next to Efteling. (VisitBrabant)

Stroopwafels — buy them fresh. The packaged version is a lie. Tara's Multicultural Table

The Preparation Kit

What to Watch, Read, Play & Listen To Before the Trip

One hundred days is enough to build proper excitement. Here's the curated list — organised by category, with everything you need to turn anticipation into an art form.

Films — Fairy Tales & Safari

These tales appear directly in Efteling's Fairytale Forest. Watch them before you walk through the real thing.

Into the Woods (2014)

Disney+ · PG · Covers Little Red Riding Hood, Cinderella, Rapunzel, Jack and the Beanstalk

Meryl Streep and Emily Blunt in a musical that covers four Efteling fairy tales in one film. Darker and more Grimm in tone — closer to what you'll see in the Sprookjesbos than Disney's usual approach. (IMDb)

Tangled (2010) / Sleeping Beauty (1959) / Snow White (1937)

Disney+ · All feature scenes in the Fairytale Forest

Rapunzel's tower, Sleeping Beauty's spinning wheel, Snow White's cottage — all are physically present in the Sprookjesbos. Efteling's versions are closer to the original Grimm tales than Disney's, which makes watching both interpretations beforehand surprisingly interesting.

The Lion King (2019 + 1994)

Disney+ · All ages

Photorealistic African savannah. The best visual primer for Beekse Bergen's lions, elephants, and giraffes. Watch both versions and compare the animation — a surprisingly good conversation with a 10-year-old.

Pirates of the Caribbean: Dead Man's Chest (2006)

Disney+ · Davy Jones and the Flying Dutchman ship appear

Context for Efteling's De Vliegende Hollander water coaster, which is based on the same Dutch legend of Captain van der Decken, cursed to sail the seas forever.

TV & Documentaries

Planet Earth II — Episode 5 (Grasslands)

Netflix / BBC iPlayer · Attenborough · Stunning 4K African savannah

The most accessible episode for Beekse Bergen prep. African savannah animals in their natural habitat — lions, wildebeest, and herds stretching to the horizon. Sets the tone perfectly.

Dynasties — "Lion" Episode

Disney+ / BBC iPlayer · Each episode follows one animal family

A lion pride fighting for survival, told as drama. Gripping format that makes kids root for specific animals. Unmissable Beekse Bergen prep.

The Imagineering Story (2019)

Disney+ · 6-part documentary

How Disney parks are designed and built. Excellent parallel viewing to Efteling content — sparks conversations about how stories become rides, and why Efteling does it differently.

Efteling: Making of Symbolica (8 episodes)

YouTube (free) · Dutch with subtitles

Behind-the-scenes series showing the design, engineering, and music production of Symbolica. Perfect for a tech-curious kid — engineers, animatronics, set design.

Books

Grimm's Complete Fairy Tales

Canterbury Classics or Penguin · All 200+ tales

Read the originals before the Fairytale Forest walk. They're darker and more atmospheric than the Disney versions, much closer to what you'll see at Efteling. Focus on the 10 Grimm tales in the Sprookjesbos: Snow White, Sleeping Beauty, Rapunzel, Hansel and Gretel, Rumpelstiltskin, The Frog King, Mother Holle, The Wolf and the Seven Kids, The Six Servants, and The Wishing-Table. (Wikipedia)

The Land of Stories: The Wishing Spell (Chris Colfer)

Ages 8–12 · Twins fall into a fairy-tale world where Grimm characters are real

A children's novel that treats fairy tales as real places you can visit — an ideal mindset to carry into Efteling. Brothers Grimm feature in later books in the series. (Series Site)

National Geographic Kids Readers: Safari

Gail Tuchman · Photo-led · Age-perfect for 10

Lions, elephants, giraffes, zebras, hyenas — all the animals you'll see at Beekse Bergen, presented with the stunning photography National Geographic does best. (Scholastic)

Games

Planet Coaster 2 (2024)

Steam Deck / PC / PS5 / Xbox · Includes a Fairytale Theme Pack

The most advanced theme park builder available. The dedicated Fairytale Theme Pack includes castle scenery, fairy-tale rides, and medieval elements — you can literally build an Efteling-style park before you visit. Runs well on Steam Deck. (Steam)

Planet Zoo + Africa Pack DLC

Steam Deck / PC · Build and manage a realistic zoo

From the same studio. The Africa Pack adds meerkats, white rhinos, African penguins, and fennec foxes. Build your own Beekse Bergen before seeing the real thing. (Steam)

RollerCoaster Tycoon Classic (2024, Switch)

Nintendo Switch · Chris Sawyer's RCT1+RCT2 remastered

The original theme park builder, brought to Switch in December 2024. Challenge: build your best coaster before arriving at Efteling. (Nintendo Store)

YouTube — The Essential Pre-Trip Watchlist

Efteling: Start with the 76-minute history deep dive by The Miracle Detective Agency, then watch POV ride videos on the official Efteling channel. Key POVs: Danse Macabre, Symbolica Treasure Tour, De Vliegende Hollander. Theme Park Worldwide has an Ultimate Guide to Efteling (March 2026) that's excellent for planning.

Beekse Bergen: "The Closest Thing to a Real Safari" (Jan 2026) covers all safari routes. "Giraffes Wash Our Car" is exactly as good as it sounds. The four Nubian giraffe calves video is irresistible at any age.

Theme park engineering: Theme Park Science has a video on how the Python was designed in collaboration with Intamin Holland. Defunctland does brilliant documentary-style dives into theme park history.

Podcasts — For the Flight

Park Rush — Episode 210: Efteling Trip Report

Spotify · UK-based theme park podcast · Family-friendly tone

Hosted by Tom and Becky. Their Efteling trip report is accessible and enthusiastic — ideal listening for the Belfast-to-Amsterdam flight.

Earth Rangers Podcast

Spotify / Apple Podcasts · Ages 6–12 · ~20 min episodes

Wild animal facts and conservation stories aimed squarely at your son's age group. Each episode is flight-length. Pair with Beekse Bergen prep.

Brains On! — Animal Episodes

Spotify / Apple Podcasts · Award-winning kids' science podcast

Episodes on elephant communication, animal vision, and migration. Accessible, funny, not condescending.

The Packing List

Everything You Need

A theme park + wildlife safari trip with a 10-year-old in Dutch summer. Nothing optional marked as essential. Nothing essential left off.

Clothing

  • Light t-shirts (4–5) — mix short and long sleeve
  • Shorts (2–3)
  • Light trousers/jeans (1–2)
  • Light cardigan or sweatshirt (1–2)
  • Waterproof/windproof packable rain jacket — ESSENTIAL, one each
  • Comfortable walking shoes/trainers — the single most important item
  • Sandals or flip-flops for hotel and hot days
  • Swimwear (Speelland water park + hotel pools)
  • Sun hat (brim, not baseball — better UV coverage)
  • Sunglasses (for both of you)
  • Underwear and socks × 7+

Theme Park Essentials

  • Small comfortable day backpack
  • Reusable water bottle — tap water is safe everywhere in NL
  • Compact folding umbrella or poncho
  • Portable phone charger / power bank
  • Spare clothes in a bag (water rides at Efteling, water park at Beekse Bergen)
  • Camera or phone with good camera; spare storage
  • Snacks from the supermarket — Dutch parks allow outside food
  • Dutch cash (€50–100) for market treats and small purchases

Safari Gear

  • Binoculars — essential for the Beekse Bergen car safari
  • Camera with zoom capability for wildlife
  • Comfortable shoes that can get muddy (walking safari terrain)
  • Spare bag for wet/dirty clothes
  • Insect repellent — useful around the safari grounds

Health & Sun

  • High-SPF sunscreen (SPF 30 or 50) — Dutch summer sun is deceptive
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Basic first aid: plasters, antiseptic wipes, antihistamine
  • Paracetamol / ibuprofen
  • Any prescription medications (plus copies of prescriptions)

Documents & Tech

  • European travel adapter (Type C/F) — Irish/UK plugs won't fit
  • Passports (valid past return date)
  • Travel insurance documents
  • Flight confirmation
  • Hotel booking confirmations (Efteling + Beekse Bergen)
  • Car rental confirmation
  • European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) or UK GHIC
  • Download before departure: 9292 app, NS app, Google Maps (offline maps for Noord-Brabant), Uber, Efteling app, Beekse Bergen 2.0 app
