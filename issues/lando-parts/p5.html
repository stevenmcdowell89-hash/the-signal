Todoist in 2026: The Features Worth Actually Using

Todoist has become one of the most copied productivity apps on Android — and yet most users run it like a basic to-do list when it's capable of significantly more. Three features that consistently surface in power-user threads as genuinely transformative:

Priority Filters + Favourites

The most effective Todoist setups use saved filters rather than projects as the daily view. A filter called "Today's Focus" — set to today & !p4 — shows your top-priority items only, stripping away the background noise. Add it as a favourite and it becomes your true daily dashboard.

Uncompletable Task Blocks

Prefix any task with * (asterisk + space) to make it uncompletable — it acts as a time block rather than a checkbox. Useful for mapping your day without over-scheduling: "* Deep work 10–12" sits alongside actual tasks and clarifies the day's shape without inflating your completion rate artificially.

The third underused feature: natural-language due dates with sections. Typing "#ProjectName /SectionName" while adding a task in Quick Add routes it directly to the right section, without opening a menu. For anyone managing several active projects, this alone reduces friction enough to justify relearning the input method.

Revolut Launches AIR — The Fintech AI Arms Race Gets Real

  • Revolut launched AIR (AI by Revolut) on April 9 — rolling out to UK customers first
  • Starling Bank introduced its own AI agent earlier this year; Monzo has similar functionality in development
  • All three neobanks have received regulatory fines in the last two years

Revolut's AIR is positioned as something beyond a chatbot. It's an embedded financial assistant that can pull spending breakdowns, track investment portfolios, manage subscriptions, block lost cards, and help plan travel budgets — all through a conversational interface, without navigating menus. The privacy positioning is deliberate: no user data is stored with third-party AI providers, and no training on customer transactions.

Revolut AIR

  • Launched April 9, UK-first rollout
  • Covers: spending insights, investments, subscriptions, card controls, travel budgeting, eSIM purchase
  • Zero data retention with external AI partners
  • No specific AI provider disclosed

Starling AI Agent

  • Launched earlier in 2026
  • Covers: account queries, spending categorisation, Spaces management
  • More conservative feature set at launch
  • Starling has stronger regulatory track record of the UK neobanks

The honest take: these features are most useful for people who don't already obsess over their finances — which is most people. If you're already using Monzo's Trends feature or Revolut's analytics tab, AIR's conversational wrapper is a nicer interface to the same data. If you're not actively managing your spending categories, it might be the nudge that actually makes the difference.

The Angle

The neobank AI race is less about the features themselves than about whether any of these companies can build the kind of trusted daily relationship that makes switching feel unthinkable. Revolut's international toolkit remains unmatched; Starling's business accounts are still the most serious in the market; Monzo's app is still the most refined. None of them has built the "everything app" yet. AIR is Revolut's best attempt at making the case that it can.
