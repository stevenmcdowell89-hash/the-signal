The Boys Are Back — and April Is Stacked

Quick Ratings
  • The Boys S5
  • Maul: Shadow Lord (eps 1–2)
  • Euphoria S3
  • The Testaments

The Boys Season 5 premiered April 8 on Prime Video, with a two-episode opening, and it lands with the weight of a show that knows this is the end. Garth Ennis's superhero satire has never been subtle — it is aggressively, magnificently unsubtle — but there's something deliberate about how these first episodes position themselves. The target list keeps expanding. The satire is eating itself in a way that feels like the point. Whether it sticks the landing over the full season will determine its legacy; the opening is as confident as the series has ever been. If you've not caught up: seasons 1–4 are on Prime Video, and Season 5 rewards having watched them.

At the same time, Euphoria Season 3 started April 8 on HBO/Sky Atlantic — the kids are back after a three-year gap, and the early reviews are cautiously warmer than the divisive Season 2. Worth monitoring if you're invested in the characters; less essential viewing for newcomers.

Star Wars: Maul — Shadow Lord

Star Wars: Maul – Shadow Lord. Episodes 3 and 4 drop tomorrow, April 13. (Disney+)

Episodes 1 and 2 of Maul — Shadow Lord arrived on April 6 to strong early notices. The animated series is set after the events of The Clone Wars and positions Maul's story with a narrative confidence that suggests the creative team have a clear destination in mind. The full 10-episode season drops in pairs every Monday through May 4 — the finale lands on Star Wars Day, which is either a deliberate piece of showmanship or an extraordinary coincidence. Almost certainly the former.

Episodes 3 and 4 — "Sinister Schemes" and "Whispers of the Unknown" — land tomorrow morning (Monday) at midnight Pacific / 8am BST.

Did You Know? Darth Maul was originally designed for The Phantom Menace (1999) to appear only in the final act — a late addition to the film. He became one of the most requested expanded universe characters despite having fewer than ten lines of dialogue in his original appearance.
