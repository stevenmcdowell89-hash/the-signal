Pokémon Champions Arrives — and It's Not What You Expected

Pokémon Champions launched April 8 across Switch and Switch 2, and it's a genuine departure from the mainline series formula. This is a pure battle game — no gyms, no story, no open world to explore. Instead: ranked and casual battles, Mega Evolution mechanics, cross-platform play with mobile (coming soon), and integration with Pokémon HOME. You bring your roster from previous games; the game gives you an arena.

The Switch 2 version runs at higher resolution and frame rates — a free upgrade for existing owners. Among the headlining Pokémon for launch: Mega Meganium (with a new sun-based Ability), Mega Emboar, and Mega Feraligatr's new "Dragonize" ability that converts Normal-type moves to Dragon. The competitive scene has already started debating whether these new abilities break existing tier structures.

Did You Know? Pokémon Champions is Nintendo's first purely competitive Pokémon title released simultaneously on Switch and Switch 2, with cross-platform matchmaking planned for mobile later this year.

Switch 2 System Update 22.1.0

Nintendo shipped a new system update this week — version 22.1.0 — following the significant 22.0.0 update in late March that introduced Handheld Boost Mode (1080p for legacy Switch games in portable mode, at the cost of battery life). This week's update focuses on stability, though Nintendo's traditionally cagey update notes leave room for hidden improvements. Worth installing.
