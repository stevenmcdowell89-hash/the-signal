The quarter-final first legs produced two significant upsets by expected standards. Bayern Munich left the Bernabéu with a 2–1 lead — the kind of away result that changes a tie — while Atlético Madrid's 2–0 destruction of Barcelona at the Camp Nou was even more comprehensive. Arsenal hold a narrow but vital 1–0 lead at Sporting. Liverpool trail PSG 2–0, a result that puts their second-leg task firmly in uncomfortable territory.

The Take

Bayern's result at Real Madrid is the headline. Both clubs have been European royalty for a decade, but this Bayern side has a quiet ruthlessness about them that the Bundesliga results don't fully capture. If Kompany's team can hold the Allianz in the second leg, they become serious semi-final threats. Atlético's performance deserves equal attention: dismantling Barcelona 2–0 away from home is the kind of result that announces a team. The second legs on April 14–15 will be unmissable.

Did You Know? PSG's 2–0 first-leg lead over Liverpool is the largest margin by either side in this fixture's Champions League history. Liverpool have won 11 previous CL ties after losing the first leg — but never by more than one goal.

Atalanta 0–1 Juventus — Boga's Hour

Jeremie Boga's 48th-minute strike was all Juventus needed at the Gewiss Stadium on Saturday evening. It was not a dominant performance — Atalanta controlled possession, won 13 corners to Juventus's two, and had Juve pinned deep for extended periods in the second half — but the defence held and Perin made the saves that were required. The three points move Juventus to 60 points and strengthen their grip on a Champions League spot. After Milan's 0–3 home defeat to Udinese on the same night, the race for fourth position is becoming genuinely interesting.

Milan's Collapse

AC Milan's 0–3 home defeat to Udinese on Saturday is the kind of result that derails title ambitions. Milan sit third on 63 points — two behind Napoli, nine behind Inter. With seven games remaining, the title looks firmly Inter's to lose. The question now is whether Milan can hold off a resurgent Napoli for second and a potentially Champions League-threatening Juventus for the final UCL berth.
