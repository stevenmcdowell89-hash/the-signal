    • The Cable Machine Question

    For anyone running a barbell-and-rack setup, the cable machine question tends to come up around year two. You've exhausted the obvious barbell movements, the kettlebell work is solid, and the missing piece is unilateral cable isolation: face pulls, lat pulldowns, single-arm rows, cable curls. The market in 2026 has good answers at every price point.

    REP Fitness Ares 2.0

    • Functional trainer attachment for existing REP PR-4000/5000 racks
    • 260lb weight stacks, upgradeable to 310lb
    • Excellent cable smoothness and versatility
    • Lifetime warranty — significantly above average
    • Higher price point; requires compatible REP rack

    Titan Fitness Functional Trainer

    • Standalone unit, dual 200lb weight stacks
    • Strong build quality, smooth cable operation
    • Works independently of existing rack setup
    • Lower warranty than REP
    • Slightly more compact footprint

    Garage Gym Reviews' current pick for home gym overall: the REP Ares 2.0 as a functional trainer attachment for those already running a REP rack; the Titan Fitness Functional Trainer as a standalone option. Both score highly for cable smoothness and versatility — the key requirements for accessory work when you don't have dumbbells in the setup.

    Kettlebell Storage — The Overlooked Problem

    Competition kettlebells take up surprising amounts of floor space when stored incorrectly and create genuine trip hazards in a small home gym. The practical solutions: purpose-built horizontal KB storage racks (available from Bells of Steel and REP from around £80–120 for a 5-bell capacity), or vertical storage A-frames (more floor footprint but easier loading). If the set runs from 12kg to 32kg, a horizontal 5-slot rack is typically the most space-efficient option.

    Recovery Toolkit
    • Foam roller — The Trigger Point GRID remains the consensus recommendation for price-to-effectiveness. Not glamorous, but consistently used beats occasionally used.
    • Lacrosse ball — Best kept by the desk. Foot arch, hip flexor, thoracic spine. Five minutes during a work break beats a dedicated session you never schedule.
    • Oura Ring Gen 4 — Garage Gym Reviews' top-rated smart fitness tracker for 2026. Better sleep staging than most wrist-based competitors; no display keeps it genuinely wearable 24/7.
    Did You Know? Competition-style kettlebells (like the ones found in most serious home gyms) maintain the same external dimensions regardless of weight — meaning a 12kg and a 32kg competition bell have identical height and handle diameter. This makes them stackable and consistent for technique work.
